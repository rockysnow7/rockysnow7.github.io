## A (Mostly) Decentralised Internet

---

The internet is a system for transmitting data between computers, with much
of that data often being stored on servers and accessed via DNS servers. The
transmission of data is handled almost entirely by ISPs. Both of these groups
hold immense power over users of the internet, having the ability to take down
the internet if they so wished. Given the social and political importance of
the internet, it would be rather problematic if these powers were exercised.

So, the following is an idea for an internet free from any devastatingly
centralised control, an outline of how it might work, and some notes on its
limitations.
